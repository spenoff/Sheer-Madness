"Sheer Madness"

GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT

by Billy Wong, Norman Deng, Spencer Nisonoff


INTRODUCTION

This document describes a game called "Sheer Madness", which is a 2D top-down puzzle solving game.


TECHNOLOGY

"Sheer Madness" will be developed using the Phaser game engine, using the Javascript programming language.


BACKSTORY

You are a dog who happens to be one of the world's best shepherds, known to be able to herd any amount of sheep through any kind of obstacles. Now you are tasked with herding sheep through some of the most difficult obstacles. Don't screw up cause your reputation is on the line.


OBJECTIVE

You are to move sheep through a series of levels, with each level becoming progressively harder and introducing new obstacles for the player to overcome.


GAMEPLAY

The game will operate with a map based system that is viewed from the top. The player will be able to move, turn, throw a lasso, or bark. The objective is to get the sheep to the goal, which will be a line of spaces or a set of spaces. Along the way, there will be a variety of obstacles that will obstruct your path to herding the sheep. Once all the sheep have been herded or lost, the level will end and a score will be given depending on the time taken, number of moves taken, and number of sheep successfully herded. A certain score is required to move on to the next level.

Player actions:

  • Move (forward/backward): Moves one space forward or backwards depending on the direction that the player is facing

  • Turn (left/right): Turns the player left or right

  • Lasso: Throws the lasso forward in the direction that the player is facing. If a sheep is in the space ahead, the sheep will be lassoed. Otherwise, the lasso is automatically retrieved.

    • Lassoing state

      • Moving: When moving forward, the sheep will be pushed one space forward in the direction you move. When moving backward, the sheep will be pulled one space backward in the direction you move. Note that if moving would cause the sheep to go into an invalid space, the player cannot move in that direction.

      • Turning: When turning, the sheep will follow in the direction that is turned, so that the sheep will always be in front of the player. Note that if turning would cause the sheep to go into an invalid space, the player cannot turn in that direction.

  • Howl: Howling moves all sheep one space away from the player. Note: the sheep can move diagonally when the player howls.

Obstacles:

  • Fences: These take up a space and prevent the player or sheep from moving onto that space.

  • Pits: If a sheep falls into a pit, that sheep will fall into the pit and be lost, lowering the possible number of points for the score. The player can also fall into the pit, in which case the player loses and has to restart that level from the beginning.

  • Wolves: They will patrol an area until a sheep gets close. When a sheep gets close, they will start to follow the sheep until it is out of range or it is eaten. If a sheep gets too close to a wolf, it will get eaten, lowering the possible number of points for the score.


CONTROLS

This game will be played with keyboard, with the following controls

  • W / Up - Move forward

  • A / Left - Turn left

  • S / Down - Move backward

  • D / Right - Turn right

  • Mouseclick - Throw lasso / Pick up lasso

  • Space - Bark


GRAPHICAL USER INTERFACE

  • Splash Screen - The splash screen will appear everytime the player launches the game. It is a simple screen with the game title that prompts the player to press any button, and it will take the player to the main menu.

  • Main Menu - The main menu will contain the game title and a simple background. It also contains 5 buttons, each with their own function.

    • START will bring the player to the first level or wherever they last saved.

    • LEVEL SELECT will bring the player to the level select menu, and the player is free to choose whichever level to play as long as they have unlocked it.

    • SETTING will bring the player to the settings menu where the player can adjust the game settings, such as volume and button controls, to their liking.

    • ABOUT will bring the player to the game's about page. The about page will give a brief background of the game along with the creators of the game.

    • QUIT will exit the player from the game.

  • IN-GAME - When the game starts the player will be given an overview of the level then moves the camera to be centered on the player character. The following items will also be shown on the player's screen

    • A button on the top left of the screen to allow the player to pause the game.

    • A timer showing the remaining time on the top center of the screen.

    • A counter to show the remaining amount of sheep in the level.

  • Pause Menu - The pause menu will contain 3 buttons.

    • RESUME will put the player back into the game.

    • SETTINGS will open the settings menu for the player and will have the same functions as described above.

    • MAIN MENU will bring the player back to the main menu.
